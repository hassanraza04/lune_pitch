Why this redesign

The product is enterprise-grade. The site should match.

A few observations, framed honestly. Nothing here is criticism of the current build. These are opportunities I'd want to invest in if I were on your team.

Today

  • Hero communicates the platform, but doesn't quickly land the headline metrics
  • The four key features (Multi-Lingual, Future-ready, Modular, Track-record) are mentioned but not visually anchored
  • Case studies list named clients without a clear at-a-glance hierarchy
  • "Schedule a Demo" is the only primary action; no secondary path for developers
  • Mobile and dark-mode polish has room to grow

This concept

  • Hero leads with three real stats animated on entry (1.5B+, 0.5ms, 99%)
  • Four key features become a dedicated section between Platform and Industries
  • Case study cards use imagery + tag + name with hover affordances
  • Two CTAs: "Schedule a Demo" for sales, "Explore products" for technical buyers
  • Dark-first design tuned to the mint accent in the logo; fully responsive
What I built

A full-site recreation, end to end.

Twelve pages, one design system, real production-grade frontend.

Design system

One stylesheet, custom-property tokens, sourced from your logo's palette.

Responsive layout

CSS Grid + Flexbox, fluid typography with clamp(), mobile-first.

Tasteful motion

Scroll reveals, animated counters, blurred sticky nav. No flashy excess.

Security by default

Strict CSP, referrer policy, honeypot form, rate-limit, strict input validation.

Beyond this static demo

What I'd build next, if we work together.

This static site is a starting point. Here's where I'd take it.

Phase 1 · Foundation

CMS & content pipeline

Wire blog and case studies to a headless CMS (Sanity / Contentful). Editors update copy without code; preview branches for stakeholder review.

Phase 1 · Foundation

Real contact + cookie banner

Wire the contact form to a real endpoint with hCaptcha and CRM hand-off. Build the interactive Cookie Preferences widget referenced today.

Phase 1 · Foundation

Brand & illustration

Replace stock photography with custom product visualizations (UI mocks, data-flow diagrams, branded illustration of the merchant graph).

Phase 2 · Conversion

Industry-specific landing

Dedicated pages per industry vertical, each with their own social proof, KPIs, and CTA. Drive paid traffic to focused pages.

Phase 2 · Conversion

Interactive product demos

Live, in-browser playground for Enrich. Paste a transaction string, see the enriched response. Self-serve qualification.

Phase 2 · Conversion

Localization

Arabic-first variant of the site (RTL, native typography). Most of your customers are in MENA. The site should meet them where they are.
