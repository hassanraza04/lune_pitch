Why this redesign

The product is enterprise-grade. The site should match.

A few observations, framed honestly. Nothing here is criticism of the current build. These are opportunities I’d want to invest in if I were on your team.

Today

  • Hero communicates the platform, but doesn’t quickly land the headline metrics
  • The four key features (Multi-Lingual, Future-ready, Modular, Track-record) are mentioned but not visually anchored
  • Case studies list named clients without a clear at-a-glance hierarchy
  • "Schedule a Demo" is the only primary action; no secondary path for developers
  • Mobile and dark-mode polish has room to grow

This concept

  • The hero shows the product itself: a ticker of raw card descriptors resolving into clean records, with the real stats (1.5B+, 0.5ms, 99%) in a ruled ledger row beneath
  • A distinct identity built for a transaction company: serif editorial headlines, mono data type, hairline-ruled sections instead of card grids
  • Case studies carry the real customer logos and link to your published stories
  • Two paths everywhere: a demo CTA for buyers, working API docs for engineers
  • Dark-first around the mint in your logo; tested across phone, tablet, and desktop
Inside this concept

A real site, not a mockup.

Everything below is live at lunepitch.pages.dev right now: the design system, the contact form, the docs, the analytics, the lot.

Cohesive design system

Tokens sourced from your logo, responsive type, dark-first. One stylesheet drives every page.

Bank-grade security

HSTS preload, strict content policies, locked-down headers. Independently verifiable on securityheaders.com.

Working contact form

Submissions hit a real backend with bot protection and rate limiting, then land in the inbox. Try it.

Privacy-first analytics

A cookie banner with real category controls. The analytics beacon only loads after the user opts in.

Search-ready SEO

Per-page metadata, social previews, structured data, sitemap. Every page is indexable and shareable.

Developer docs

A reference-style docs section with endpoint pages and copy-paste code samples in four languages.

Accessibility & performance

Keyboard navigation, visible focus, reduced-motion support, lazy images, no layout shift. Tested at every breakpoint.

Restrained motion

One signature animation (the enrichment ticker), quick fades, a sticky nav. Everything stops when the user asks it to.

What I’d add next

The shortlist, with you.

A working concept is the starting point. Here’s the work that turns it into a production marketing site, grouped by what each piece needs.

Brand & content

Needs brand files

Real customer & certification logos

Replace the text-pill "Trusted by" strip with actual customer marks (ADIB, NBF, D360, Beyon Money, Alaan Pay), and use the official Dubai AI Seal, SOC, and ISO 27001 logos in the Certified By block.

Needs real numbers

Outcome metrics on every case study

One quantified result per customer story: engagement uplift, time to deploy, transactions processed. The 98% categorization stat from the ADIB story is already on the home page; the rest need real numbers from Lune’s deployments.

Needs product assets

Real product UI on the products page

Swap the illustrated mocks for actual screenshots of the Enrich dashboard, the Engage SDK in a banking app, and the Target campaign builder. Stock photography goes the same way.

Needs counsel review

Final legal & cookie policy

The cookie banner, consent gating, and preference modal are all live. Just need your counsel’s published Privacy, Terms, and Cookie text to drop in.

Editor experience

Headless CMS for blog & case studies

Wire the content surfaces to a CMS like Sanity, Contentful, or Hygraph. Marketing can publish without engineering, with preview branches for review before go-live.

Product depth

Hero loop

Animated enrichment in the hero

A short, looping animation that shows a raw transaction string becoming a structured record: category, merchant, location, carbon. Replaces decorative geometry with the product itself.

Needs API access

Live "Try Enrich" sandbox

Paste a transaction string, see the enriched response from a sandboxed test key. The strongest possible self-serve qualifier for technical buyers.

Per-vertical pages

Industry landing pages

Standalone pages for Retail Banks, Neo-Banks & Wallets, and Fintechs, each with their own social proof, outcome numbers, and CTA. Tailored destinations for paid acquisition.

Region match

Arabic & RTL variant

A full RTL variant of the site in IBM Plex Sans Arabic. Most of Lune’s customers are in MENA, so the site should meet them where they are.

Site search

⌘K command palette

Global keyboard search across docs, case studies, and product pages. The kind of detail serious technical buyers notice immediately.

Booking

Calendar on the contact page

Embed Cal.com or Calendly so prospects can book a 20-minute walkthrough without waiting for an email reply. The form stays as the fallback path.

Operations

Domain & email

Move onto Lune’s own domain

Point lunedata.io (or a chosen subdomain) at the deploy, configure SPF / DKIM / DMARC, and switch the contact form to send from a real Lune address.

External audit

Penetration test

A real pen test by an external firm before any production customer is onboarded. The scope is already documented in the audits folder.

Live monitoring

Error tracking & uptime

Sentry for error tracking, Better Stack for uptime, Lighthouse budgets in CI, and dependency scanning. The static-scan script in the repo is the starting point.
